Six Fun Sheep Facts:

1) According to the Guiness Book of World Records, the oldest sheep lived to be 23 years old.

2) A sheep's wool will grow forever.

3) Sheep have nearly 360 degree vision.

4) Sheep can't get up is they are on their back.

5) An 'Ewe' is a female sheep, and a 'Ram' is a male sheep.

6) The average life expectancy of sheep is about 10 to 12 years.

    Different Types of Sheep

Merino

The Merino is one of the most historically relevant and economically influential breeds of sheep, very prized for its wool.

Dorset Horn

Dorsets are white sheep with open faces and long bodies. Ewes weigh 150–200 pounds, and rams weigh 225–275 pounds. The sheep are avid grazers and use feed well.

Border Leicester

The Border Leicester is a British breed of sheep. It is a polled, long-wool sheep and is considered a dual-purpose breed as it is reared both for meat and for wool. The sheep are large but docile.

Leicester Longwool

The Leicester Longwool is an English breed of sheep. Alternative names for the breed include: Leicester, Bakewell Leicester, Dishley Leicester, English Leicester, Improved Leicester and New Leicester.

Southwood Sheep

The Southdown is a small, dual-purpose English sheep, raised primarily for meat. The Southdown breed was originally bred by John Ellman of Glynde, near Lewes, East Sussex, about 200 years ago.

Texel Sheep

The Texel is a breed of domestic sheep originally from the island of Texel in the Netherlands. A heavily muscled sheep, it produces a lean meat carcass and will pass on this quality to crossbred progeny.

Jacob Sheep

The Jacob is a British breed of domestic sheep. It combines two characteristics unusual in sheep: it is piebald—dark-coloured with areas of white wool—and it is often polycerate or multi-horned. It most commonly has four horns.

Corriedale

Corriedale sheep are a dual purpose breed, meaning they are used both in the production of wool and meat.

Rambouillet Sheep

The Rambouillet is a breed of sheep also known as the Rambouillet Merino or the French Merino. The development of the Rambouillet breed started in 1786, when Louis XVI purchased over 300 Spanish Merinos from his cousin, King Charles III of Spain.
